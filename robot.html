The Technology

What is the CORI Surgical System?

The CORI Surgical System by Smith+Nephew is the world's only handheld robotic-assisted platform for both partial and total knee replacement — and the first robotic system indicated for revision knee surgery. Unlike large robotic arm systems, the CORI is compact, portable, and designed around the surgeon's natural movements.

At the heart of the CORI is Real Intelligence (RI) software — an AI-powered planning tool that creates a 3D digital model of your knee during surgery itself. No pre-operative CT scan or MRI is required. The system maps your unique bone surfaces intra-operatively, allowing Dr. Allahbadia to plan and execute your surgery with sub-millimeter precision, entirely customised to your anatomy.

The CORI's ergonomic handheld burr has an automatic stop feature: it immediately ceases cutting when outside the pre-planned surgical boundary, ensuring no healthy bone is ever inadvertently removed. The result is a surgery that is both more precise and inherently safer than traditional methods.

How the CORI System Works

From consultation to recovery — a step-by-step guide to your robotic knee replacement journey with Dr. Allahbadia.

Dr. Allahbadia reviews your symptoms, X-rays, and knee function. He determines whether total or partial knee replacement — or another procedure — is the right approach for your specific anatomy and lifestyle goals.

Intra-Operative 3D Mapping

In the operating theatre, Dr. Allahbadia uses the CORI's tracking pointer to map your knee's bone surfaces in real time. The system builds a precise 3D model of your unique anatomy — no pre-surgical CT or MRI required. This is called "image-agnostic smart mapping."

The CORI's RI.KNEE AI software presents Dr. Allahbadia with a personalised surgical plan — implant size, positioning, alignment targets — powered by AI reference values. Handheld Robotic Bone Preparation

Dr. Allahbadia operates the CORI's ergonomic handheld burr to remove damaged bone with sub-millimeter accuracy. The robotic system continuously monitors his movements and automatically stops the burr if it approaches the boundary of the surgical plan — eliminating human error from bone preparation.

The perfectly prepared bone surfaces receive the implant — chosen from Smith+Nephew's widest available range, including options with OXINIUM Oxidized Zirconium, a ceramicised metal alloy twice as hard as cobalt chrome, designed for maximum longevity and minimal wear.

Your Questions Answered

FAQ: CORI Robotic Surgery

Does the robot perform my surgery?

add
No. Dr. Allahbadia performs your surgery entirely. The CORI system is a precision tool that assists him — like a highly intelligent saw guide. It provides real-time feedback, safety stops, and sub-millimeter accuracy, but every decision and every movement is made by Dr. Allahbadia. The robot enhances your surgeon's skill; it does not replace it.

Do I need a CT scan or MRI before robotic surgery?

add
No. The CORI is an "image-agnostic" system — it does not require pre-operative CT or MRI scans. Instead, it maps your knee in three dimensions during the surgery itself, in real time. You will only need standard X-rays for your consultation with Dr. Allahbadia.

Is robotic knee surgery more painful?

add
On the contrary — robotic-assisted surgery typically results in less post-operative pain than traditional surgery. Because the CORI allows for sub-millimeter precision in bone preparation, there is less trauma to surrounding soft tissues. Patients treated by Dr. Allahbadia regularly report faster mobilisation and earlier discharge than they anticipated.

Can CORI be used for revision (repeat) knee surgery?

add
Yes — the CORI is the first robotic system in the world to receive indication for revision knee arthroplasty. Its image-free registration means it can map anatomy even after an existing implant has been removed, with no risk of image distortion from in-situ metal components. Dr. Allahbadia specialises in complex revision cases precisely because of this capability.

How long will my new knee last?

add
With accurate implant placement — which the CORI ensures — and the option to use OXINIUM Oxidized Zirconium implants (twice as hard and far more wear-resistant than standard cobalt chrome), your knee replacement can be expected to last significantly longer than traditional surgery outcomes. Most patients can expect 20+ years of function with appropriate care.

How do I know if I'm a candidate for robotic knee replacement?

add
The best way is to book a consultation with Dr. Allahbadia at Hinduja Healthcare Surgical, Khar. Bring your X-rays and any available imaging. Dr. Allahbadia will assess your knee, review your lifestyle and activity goals, and recommend the most appropriate treatment — which may or may not include robotic surgery. Not every knee condition requires surgery, and Dr. Allahbadia always explores conservative options first.
