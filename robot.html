Background

What is the CORI Robotic Surgical System?

Launched in July 2020 after FDA clearance, CORI™ by Smith+Nephew evolved from their earlier NAVIO system — smaller, faster, and more precise. Unlike arm-mounted robots, CORI is held directly by the surgeon, combining robotic accuracy with human dexterity.

The CORI Surgical system itself is an advanced, image-free handheld robotic platform developed to assist orthopedic surgeons in executing highly precise total knee, partial knee, revision knee, and total hip replacement procedures.

Unlike older, bulkier robotic technologies that rely on massive mechanical arms, CORI utilizes compact, software-driven handheld instrumentation. It gives the combined benefits of computer-guided precision and complete tactile control during bone preparation.

Patients Benefit when opted for robotic assisted surgery as these require "Zero Pre-OP Radiation" in terms of CT Scans, Promoted to Preserve Healthy Tissue and all over faster recovery to get back to daily pain-free life.

Dr. Vivek Allahbadia was among the first surgeons in Mumbai to adopt the system and operates it exclusively at Hinduja Healthcare Surgical, Khar.

Step by Step

How CORI Works

1

Bone landmark registration

At the start of surgery, Dr. Allahbadia uses the CORI probe to register specific bone landmarks. RI.KNEE AI software instantly constructs a 3D digital model of your unique knee anatomy.

2

Digital Tensioner — objective balance data

Before any bone is cut, the Digital Tensioner measures ligament laxity and joint gap. Objective numerical data replaces surgeon feel on the most critical step.

3

Personalised implant plan

Dr. Allahbadia selects the exact implant size, position, and alignment on-screen — matched to your bone structure, not a population average.

4

Robotic-guided bone resection

The handheld robotic burr executes the plan with 300+ feedback signals per second. An automatic safety stop activates if the tool approaches the boundary of the planned zone.

5

Implant confirmed and placed

System confirms placement matches the plan exactly. First-time accuracy means minimal soft tissue disruption — the primary driver of fast recovery.

FAQs

Common Questions

Is CORI an autonomous robot?

No — the surgeon controls every movement. CORI guides and prevents unsafe cuts but never acts independently.

I've been refused by other surgeons — could CORI help?

The CORI Robotic System was made to tackle such complex cases. And under skilled hands of Dr. Allahbadia even complex, high-BMI, and revision cases can be tackled with proper guidance and ease.

What makes Dr. Allahbadia's approach different?

He combines 28 years of hands-on arthroplasty experience with the world's most advanced handheld robotic platform — and a published technique for high-risk patients that no other Mumbai surgeon has matched.

Will it cost more than conventional surgery?

The upfront cost is higher. However, a shorter hospital stay, faster return to work, and lower revision rates typically reduce the total lifetime cost of care.

Is CORI suitable for partial knee replacement?

Yes — CORI supports unicompartmental (partial) and total knee replacement. Partial replacement preserves healthy bone and ligaments, often producing a more natural-feeling knee.

What does recovery actually look like?

Most patients stand the day after surgery, go home the following morning, and return to daily activities within 2–3 weeks — with minimal pain throughout, because robotic precision reduces soft tissue trauma.
