Overview

What is CORI
Robotic Surgery?

The Smith+Nephew CORI Surgical System helps the surgeon place your knee implant with accuracy that manual instruments simply cannot match. The surgeon does everything — the robot makes them more precise.

"Think of it as GPS with safety boundaries for surgery — you can only cut where the plan says you should."

CORI is imageless — unlike older robots, no pre-operative CT scan or MRI is needed. It builds a live 3D model of your knee right there in the operating theatre.

The Procedure

What Happens
Step by Step

Every movement verified. Every cut planned. No guesswork.

01

Anaesthesia

Spinal or general anaesthesia. Most patients at Hinduja Khar receive spinal — you are comfortable and alert, and recovery is faster.

02

Tracker Placement

Tiny sensors are fixed to the thigh and shin bones. They communicate with the CORI system continuously — giving the computer your leg's exact 3D position in real time.

03

Live Knee Mapping

Dr. Allahbadia uses a probe to register bone landmarks. The CORI system instantly builds a live 3D model of your unique knee anatomy — no CT scan, no pre-op radiation.

04

On-Screen Planning

The surgeon plans the exact implant size, position, and alignment on-screen — customised entirely to your anatomy, not a population average.

05
Core Step

Robotic-Guided Bone Cutting

The handheld robotic burr executes the plan with 300+ feedback signals per second. It automatically stops if the tool approaches the boundary of the planned zone. Sub-millimetre accuracy on every cut.

06

Trial Balancing

Temporary components are inserted and the knee is tested through full range of motion. The Digital Tensioner measures ligament tension objectively. Adjustments made before the final implant goes in.

07

Final Implant Insertion

Metal femoral component, metal tibial tray, polyethylene insert — permanently fixed. First-time accuracy means minimal soft tissue disruption. Walk the next day.

The surgeon decides.

Common Questions

Questions
Patients Ask.

Everything you need to know before making a decision. If your question isn't here — bring it to the consultation.

Book a Consultation arrow_forward
"Will the robot operate on me alone?"add
Absolutely not. The surgeon controls every movement. CORI guides accuracy and prevents unsafe cuts — but it cannot act independently. It is a precision instrument, not an autonomous machine.
"Is robotic surgery safer?"add
It may improve precision and consistency. However, no surgery is risk-free. The risks of anaesthetic complications, infection, and DVT exist regardless of technique. What CORI reduces is the risk of long-term implant failure due to malalignment.
"Will recovery be faster?"add
Often, yes — less soft tissue damage = less pain. Most CORI patients at Hinduja Khar walk the day after surgery and return to daily activities in 2–3 weeks. Physiotherapy is still essential.
"Is it more expensive?"add
Yes — robotic systems carry a higher capital cost. However, a shorter hospital stay, faster return to work, and lower risk of revision surgery frequently offset this over time.
"I've been refused elsewhere — could I still be a candidate?"add
Quite possibly. Dr. Allahbadia regularly accepts complex, high-BMI, and revision cases. CORI's intraoperative mapping adapts to any anatomy. A consultation is the only way to know for certain.
"How long does the implant last?"add
15–25 years or more, depending on weight, activity, implant quality, alignment, and surgeon technique. Accurate alignment — which CORI improves — directly reduces uneven wear and extends implant life.
